Kinetic energy spectra in buoyancy driven bubbly flows

Buoyancy driven bubbly flows are found in many industrial and natural places. In a suspension of bubbles, the interaction of wakes due to rising bubbles can result in a complex flow structures similar to turbulence. Earlier experiments and simulations on homogeneous swarm of bubbly flows have revealed that the kinetic energy spectra scales as \(k^{-3}\) in both frequency and wave number space. The underlying mechanism for the scaling have been attributed to the dissipation of the energy injected due to buoyancy by viscous forces. We using scale-by-scale budget analysis show that, for scales smaller than the bubble diameter, the net transfer because of the surface tension and the non-linear flux balances viscous dissipation to give $k^{-3}$ scaling in the kinetic energy spectrum.
